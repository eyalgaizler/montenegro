How to read this plan

A gentle loop, three bases, the same calm rhythm every day

Every day runs on the rhythm you asked for: a morning out, a real midday rest (14:00–16:00) back near the hotel, then a late-afternoon / evening — with the car put away by 19:00 and a relaxed on-foot evening until about 22:00. No single drive runs over an hour, and we break at least once an hour on the road.

Star trip or round trip? — what we landed on

Neither extreme. A pure star trip (one hotel all week) would force daily round-trips well past your one-hour driving limit, because Montenegro is long and narrow. A full round trip (a new hotel most nights) means packing and unpacking constantly — tiring at this pace.

So this is a gentle 2–3 base loop: three nights on the Bay of Kotor, three on the Budva Riviera, two in Tivat by the airport — only two hotel changes, every hop under an hour, and the trip ends a ten-minute drive from your flight home.

Before you go — the fine print that matters

Practical notes

The loop & the driving rules

Three bases, two hotel changes: Bay of Kotor (3 nights) → Budva Riviera (3) → Tivat (2). Every leg — airport→Kotor, Kotor→Budva, Budva→Tivat, and each day trip — is under an hour, and we break at least once an hour on the road. The trip deliberately ends in Tivat, minutes from your flight.

Heads-up: summer roadworks at the Vrmac tunnel can slow the Kotor–Budva–Tivat stretch — leave with a buffer, never a rush.

Pacing for the group

Everything is built around the 14:00–16:00 rest and gentle, flat routes. We skip the strenuous bits on purpose — the Kotor city-walls climb and the dizzying Lovćen serpentine — and lean on seated boats and short drives instead. On the Lake Skadar day the rest becomes a long lakeside lunch.

Sveti Stefan — view only

The iconic island resort (Aman Sveti Stefan) has been closed since 2020 and you can't go onto the island. The famous picture is the view from the mainland at Miločer — which is the best angle anyway, and very easy to reach.

Hotels & booking

The picks are boutique 3–4★ (with one 5★ marina option in Tivat if you want to splurge at the end). Late August/early September is high season on the coast — book the three bases well ahead, and choose places with their own parking since you'll have the car.

Car & parking

Old towns (Kotor, Budva, Perast) are pedestrian — park in the lots just outside the walls and walk the flat lanes. Roads are good and scenic; fuel and tolls are modest (the Sozina tunnel toll is a couple of euros). An automatic car is worth requesting for comfort.

Money, season & the date

Montenegro uses the euro; cards are widely taken but carry some cash for konobas and boats. Early September is warm and lovely with thinner crowds than August.
